Ideas Worth Testing

First-Scroll AI-Visible Messaging Test

Test whether the hero copy variant that performs best in A/B tests on the website also produces the highest brand presence in AI search summaries (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) when the company/product category is queried. Compare the messaging attributes of website-winning variants vs. AI-visible variants.

Hypothesis: The messaging that converts best on-site (specificity, proof, urgency) may differ from the messaging that gets surfaced in AI summaries (authority signals, category framing, unique POV). If true, CRO practitioners need a bifurcated messaging strategy — one for on-site conversion, one for pre-click AI visibility.

UGC vs. Feature Description Conversion Test (SaaS Product Pages)

A/B test product pages with pure UGC (customer quotes with specific workflow/outcome detail) against pages with feature-description copy and generic testimonials. Measure signup rate, activation rate at day-7, and paid conversion.

Hypothesis: Based on Talia Wolf's cited 74% lift for UGC pages: SaaS buyers need to see themselves in the product, not just hear about the product. Real workflow-in-context UGC will outperform feature-list copy at both the signup and activation stage, because it resolves the 'will this work for someone like me?' objection before the trial starts.

Activation-First Pricing Experiment

For a SaaS product with a trial or freemium model: test removing the paywall entirely on the highest-value feature (the 'aha moment' feature) for the first 14 days, vs. standard gated trial. Measure activation rate, word-of-mouth referral rate, and free-to-paid conversion at day-30.

Hypothesis: Based on Verna's Lovable freemium thesis: treating AI/high-value feature cost as marketing spend rather than COGS will increase activation and word-of-mouth sufficiently to offset the margin hit. The compounding conversion lift from higher activation outperforms the margin impact of an ungated first period.

Social-Intent vs. Search-Intent Landing Page Messaging Split

Route social media traffic (Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter) and search traffic (branded + non-branded) to separate landing page variants. Social variant leads with emotional resonance and 'I am someone who...' identity framing. Search variant leads with specific, outcome-oriented, comparison-aware copy. Measure conversion rate by source, not blended.

Hypothesis: Israel's known view: traffic intent (social vs. search) is critical for messaging. Blending traffic into a single landing page means the messaging is wrong for at least 50% of visitors. Splitting by intent and tailoring the first scroll to intent type will produce higher aggregate conversion than any single optimized variant.
