Ideas Worth Testing

Post-AI-Answer Confirmation Test

Segment landing page visitors by source: AI-referred/branded/direct (post-research) vs. cold paid/social (pre-research). Test two hero section variants: one that educates (assumes awareness is low) vs. one that confirms/validates the decision already being made (assumes high awareness). Measure CVR, scroll depth, and time-to-CTA-click separately for each segment.

Hypothesis: Visitors arriving post-AI-Overview or via branded search have already formed a preliminary decision. Messaging that immediately validates and accelerates that decision (social proof, specificity, risk reduction) will convert at higher rates for this segment than messaging designed to build awareness. Messaging designed for cold traffic will outperform for paid/social visitors.

Offensive Test Quota Experiment

Deliberately restructure the A/B testing backlog so that 40-50% of queued tests are classified as 'offensive' (new messaging angles, new value proposition framings, new user segments, new conversion paths) rather than defensive (friction reduction, form optimization, button color). Run both queues in parallel over a quarter and measure not win rate but expected revenue impact per test.

Hypothesis: A backlog rebalanced toward offensive tests will produce fewer wins but higher average impact per winning test, resulting in greater total revenue impact per quarter than a purely defensive testing program. The loss rate increase is the cost of accessing higher-ceiling bets.

Emotional Trigger vs. Feature Proof Hero Test

On a high-traffic landing page, test: (A) current feature-led hero (lists capabilities, screenshots, metrics), (B) emotionally-led hero focused on the pain state before the product, written using customer language mined from reviews/Reddit/support tickets. Measure CVR and downstream activation/trial-to-paid rates.

Hypothesis: In a world where AI search has pre-answered feature questions for arriving visitors, messaging that resonates with emotional triggers (the state they're trying to escape or achieve) will outperform feature-proof messaging for high-intent visitors. Emotional alignment reduces the gap between 'this product does what I need' and 'I trust that this is right for me.'

Activation-First Experiment Sequencing Audit

Audit the current experiment roadmap and classify every test by the growth lever it addresses: activation, monetization, acquisition, retention. If the distribution is skewed away from activation, run a 90-day sprint where activation tests get priority queue status. Compare overall program revenue impact before and after rebalancing.

Hypothesis: Following Verna's activation-first sequencing thesis: if activation is the root disease behind conversion and retention problems, a testing program that prioritizes activation experiments will generate compounding improvements across all downstream metrics, producing higher total revenue impact than a balanced or acquisition-weighted testing program.
