Ideas Worth Testing

Traffic-Source Segmented First-Scroll Test

Run the same landing page variation against three traffic segments separately: organic SEO, AI-referred (ChatGPT/Perplexity/SGE), and paid social. Measure not just conversion rate but scroll depth, time-on-page, and click intent (CTA vs. navigation).

Hypothesis: AI-referred visitors arrive with higher prior product awareness and different intent than SEO visitors, meaning the optimal hero message and CTA for each segment are structurally different. A single 'winning' variant may be averaging two opposite user behaviors.

Cancellation Flow Step-Level Conversion Audit

Instrument every micro-step in the cancellation flow (intent → reason survey → rescue offer → confirmation) and run A/B tests on the rescue intervention timing and format (e.g., immediate pause offer vs. delayed win-back email vs. personalized downgrade path based on usage signals).

Hypothesis: The cancellation flow is an untested high-intent conversion surface. Treating each step as an experiment with 'save rate' as the primary metric will reveal that most churn is recoverable at a specific friction point, not at the final cancel click.

Clarity-Before-Compelling Homepage Hero Test

Test two hero variants: (A) current 'compelling' hero with benefit-led headline, (B) 'clarity-first' hero that leads with category and use-case before differentiation. Measure scroll depth to value prop section, form engagement, and qualified lead rate — not just click-through.

Hypothesis: Visitors who receive clarity-first messaging will scroll further and convert to qualified leads at a higher rate because they self-select accurately — fewer low-intent clicks, more high-intent engagement.

Off-Page Presence Impact on On-Page Conversion

For a subset of B2B landing pages, systematically update messaging on third-party review sites (G2, Capterra), Reddit threads, and AI-generated summaries (via AEO/GEO content), then measure whether on-page conversion rate shifts for AI-referred traffic over a 30-day window.

Hypothesis: On-page conversion rate for AI-referred traffic is not a function of on-page elements alone — it's a function of the prior context formed on AI platforms. Improving off-page messaging will lift on-page conversion for AI-referred visitors without changing the page.

Activation-First vs. Acquisition-First Experiment Portfolio Rebalancing

Audit the current A/B test backlog: categorize each test by funnel stage (acquisition, activation, monetization, retention). If activation tests are underrepresented, run a 60-day sprint where 70% of new test slots are activation-focused. Measure downstream impact on free-to-paid conversion and 30-day retention.

Hypothesis: Per Elena Verna's sequencing model, an underinvestment in activation experiments creates a false ceiling on acquisition CRO results. Fixing activation-stage friction will produce compounding lifts that acquisition-stage tests alone cannot achieve.
